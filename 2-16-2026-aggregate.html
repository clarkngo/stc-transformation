Executive Briefing: BSAI Program Launch

Goal: Launch a market-leading BS in Artificial Intelligence that prioritizes "Autonomy and Action" over traditional "Insights and Inference."

1. The Strategic Pivot

We are shifting from a theory-heavy curriculum to an Engineering-First model. This includes replacing standalone Generative AI core requirements with a rigorous Machine Learning & Data Mining foundation to ensure students can build the underlying "logic engines" for autonomous agents.

SUBJECT: Transitioning CS 251 to Python-First Engineering & Core Realignment

Executive Summary

To align with the 2026 AI Engineer Roadmap, we propose transitioning CS 251 (Statistical Computing) from R to a Python-centric "Computational Mathematics" framework. This shift addresses the "Math Gap" by bridging theoretical linear algebra with production-level engineering. Furthermore, the realignment from Generative AI to Machine Learning & Data Mining ensures our graduates possess the foundational rigor required for high-stakes roles in Cybersecurity and Operational Technology (OT).

The Rationale: Why Python over R?

While R is excellent for statistical visualization, the 2026 industry standard for MLOps and Agentic Systems is exclusively Python-based. Teaching math in Python allows students to master Tensors—the primary data structure for Neural Networks—months before they reach upper-division labs.

Pros of Python-First:
  • Seamless transition to DS 479 (Deep Learning).
  • Industry alignment (OpenAI, Meta, DeepMind).
  • Focus on GPU-accelerated "Vectorized" math.
Cons of R-Based Approach:
  • Limited deep learning ecosystem.
  • Misalignment with industry hiring trends.
  • Students must "relearn" with Python in labs.

Executive Summary: AI-Assisted Cyber Curricular Development

Recent initiatives funded by the National Science Foundation and the National Security Agency have established new frameworks for integrating Artificial Intelligence into cybersecurity education. Research indicates that AI can significantly accelerate the development of Knowledge Units while maintaining high levels of cognitive rigor.

1. Key Research Insights

  • Accelerated Timeline: Custom AI assistants helped compress a multi-year development process into a six-month iterative cycle.
  • Cognitive Rigor: AI-assisted learning outcomes are on par with or exceed established frameworks, particularly in aligning with Bloom's taxonomy for higher-order thinking.
  • Collaborative Strengths: AI is highly effective at generating educationally sound descriptions and learning outcomes that often required only minimal human revision.
  • Emphasis on Application: AI-generated outcomes tend to favor the "Apply and Analyze" cognitive taxon rather than just basic memorization.

2. Action Plan: Leveraging BSAI Courses for CY Programs

To align CityU's Cybersecurity programs with the new CyberAI Program of Study validation requirements, the following steps are proposed:

A. Curriculum Integration (BSAI to CY)

  • Foundational Alignment: Integrate BSAI courses to satisfy the AI Foundational Knowledge Units: AI Governance, Laws, and Ethics; AI Fundamentals; and Machine Learning Fundamentals.
  • Specialization Tracks:
    • SecureAI Track: Utilize BSAI coursework in Advanced Math for AI, Securing the AI Lifecycle, Deep Learning, and Adversarial Learning.
    • AICyber Track: Utilize BSAI coursework in AI for Security Assessment and Defensive or Offensive Applications of AI.

B. Validation Compliance Requirements

  • Hands-on Competency: Ensure all aligned BSAI courses include documented applied lab exercises that develop specific competencies, as required for validation.
  • Work Role Mapping: Align the program with relevant cybersecurity work roles from the NICE Framework or DoD Cyber Workforce Framework to bridge the gap between education and employment.
  • Student Recognition: Establish a process for official transcript notation or certificates recognizing completion of a validated Program of Study.

C. Faculty & Program Maintenance

  • Continuous Improvement: Implement a regular evaluation schedule to assess Program-Level Learning Outcomes and adjust the curriculum based on performance metrics.
  • Faculty Support: Maintain updated faculty CVs highlighting AI and cybersecurity research, industry involvement, and sponsorship of student activities.

Program Philosophy: "Autonomy & Action"

The BSAI program is built on three integrated pillars:

🏗️ The Engineering Foundation Mastering the "silicon-to-cloud" pipeline: Operating Systems, Networks, Cloud Architecture, and Infrastructure. Students learn how intelligence integrates with system constraints.

🧠 Cognitive Architecture Semantic intelligence using Natural Language Processing, Deep Learning, Transformers, and Vector Databases. Focus on representation learning and reasoning engines.

🤖 The Agentic Edge MLOps/AgentOps and Physical AI—bridging digital intelligence with robotics, IoT, and industrial systems through Digital Twins and Operational Technology (OT) integration.

Curriculum Structure: 180 Credit Hours

Preparatory Track: Python-First & Math Bridge

Students begin with a computational foundation that bridges traditional CS fundamentals with AI-specific mathematical concepts:

Course Title Strategic Purpose
CS 132 Computer Science I Logic & Java foundations—core object-oriented thinking
IS 201 Fundamentals of Computing The Python "Bridge"—intermediate Python, OOP, and ecosystem introduction
CS 251 Statistical Computing CRITICAL INNOVATION: Computational Math using Python/Tensors. Teaches Linear Algebra, Probability, Vector Calculus entirely through NumPy. Replaces R-based statistics.

Core Engineering Requirements (8 courses)

Foundational competencies required across all AI engineering roles, with emphasis on systems integration and secure development:

  • CS 320: Fundamentals of AI (Classical ML)
  • CS 330: Operating Systems (Critical for Physical AI/Edge Computing)
  • CS 340: Computer Networks (Distributed AI, IoT Integration)
  • CS 469: Data Structures & Algorithms (Integrated with Discrete Math)
  • IS 345: Cybersecurity Foundations (Secure coding, threat modeling)
  • IS 360: Database Technologies (Focus on Vector DBs for RAG)
  • IS 471: Cyber & AI Ethics (Responsible AI, NSA alignment)
  • IT 434: Cloud Computing (MLOps, deployment patterns)

Depth of Study: Artificial Intelligence (60 Credits)

Upper-division courses focusing on advanced AI systems, agentic architecture, and production deployment:

  • AI 410: AI Engineering—System design, model serving, production pipelines
  • AI 420: Agentic Systems—Autonomous agents, planning, LLM orchestration
  • AI 430: MLOps & AgentOps—Model monitoring, versioning, operational excellence
  • AI 440: Physical AI & Digital Twins—Robotics, IoT, industrial systems integration
  • DS 479: Data Mining & Deep Learning—Deep networks, CNNs, Transformers, backpropagation

Strategic Constraints & Design Goals

1. The Math Gap Resolution

We have moved away from R to a Python-first math approach. Linear Algebra and Calculus are taught entirely through Tensor manipulation in Python, ensuring students:

  • Understand matrix operations and eigenvalue decomposition
  • Master tensor broadcasting for deep learning
  • Are prepared for GPU acceleration and automatic differentiation
  • Can implement backpropagation without additional remediation

2. Operational Technology (OT) Integration

Unlike standard CS degrees, we specifically target the intersection of AI and OT—a high-growth "blue ocean" market. Coursework in Physical AI and Digital Twins prepares graduates for roles in industrial robotics, smart infrastructure, and critical systems security.

3. NSA/CyberAI Validation

The program must align with NSA SecureAI pathway requirements, including documented rigor in:

  • Adversarial Learning and attack vectors
  • Securing the AI Lifecycle
  • Advanced Mathematics for AI
  • AI Governance and Ethics

4. Market Hireability

Graduates are positioned for roles at:

  • AI Labs: OpenAI, Meta AI Research, DeepMind, Anthropic
  • Industrial AI: Boston Dynamics, Tesla, Siemens
  • Defense & Intelligence: NSA, DoD contractors
  • Cloud AI Platforms: AWS, Azure, Google Cloud
