BS in Artificial Intelligence (BSAI): Program Philosophy

Role & Objective: Design an expert-level curriculum for a Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence at City University of Seattle. The program produces "Action-Ready" AI Engineers for the 2026 labor market while meeting NSA/NCAE-C CyberAI validation standards.

The Three Pillars: "Autonomy & Action"

Unlike traditional Data Science programs that focus on insights and dashboards, BSAI emphasizes building autonomous, real-time engines for production environments.

🏗️ The Engineering Foundation Mastering the "silicon-to-cloud" pipeline: Operating Systems, Networks, Cloud Architecture, and Infrastructure. Students learn how intelligence integrates with system constraints.
🧠 Cognitive Architecture Semantic intelligence using Natural Language Processing, Deep Learning, Transformers, and Vector Databases. Focus on representation learning and reasoning engines.
🤖 The Agentic Edge MLOps/AgentOps and Physical AI—bridging digital intelligence with robotics, IoT, and industrial systems through Digital Twins and Operational Technology (OT) integration.

Target Roles & Market Positioning

Graduates are prepared for four primary career paths:

  • AI Engineer: Full-stack ML systems in production
  • ML Engineer: Algorithm development and optimization
  • Physical AI Specialist: Robotics, IoT, industrial automation
  • Agentic Systems Architect: Autonomous agent design and deployment

Curriculum Structure: 180 Credit Hours

Preparatory Track: Python-First & Math Bridge

Students begin with a computational foundation that bridges traditional CS fundamentals with AI-specific mathematical concepts.

CS 132 Computer Science I (Logic & Java Foundations) — Core logic, object-oriented thinking, and foundational algorithms using Java as the syntax vehicle.
IS 201 Fundamentals of Computing (The Python Bridge) — Intermediate Python, OOP patterns, libraries (NumPy, Pandas), and ecosystem introduction. Bridges Java to production Python.
CS 251 Statistical Computing (Computational Math) — Critical Innovation: Teaches Linear Algebra, Probability, and Vector Calculus entirely through Python/NumPy Tensors. Students master GPU-accelerated matrix operations before reaching Deep Learning labs. Replaces R-based statistics with production-ready Python.

Core Engineering Requirements

These courses provide the foundational competencies required across all AI engineering roles.

CS 320 Fundamentals of AI — Classical machine learning (supervised/unsupervised), decision trees, ensemble methods, and foundational ML theory.
CS 330 Operating Systems — Critical for Physical AI and edge computing. Process management, memory hierarchy, and real-time constraints.
CS 340 Computer Networks — Essential for distributed AI, IoT integration, and cloud-edge communication patterns.
CS 469 Data Structures & Algorithms — Integrated with Discrete Mathematics. Complexity analysis, graph algorithms for knowledge graphs, and optimization techniques.
IS 345 Cybersecurity Foundations — Secure coding, threat modeling, and cryptographic concepts essential for securing AI systems.
IS 360 Database Technologies — Focus on Vector Databases (Pinecone, Weaviate, Milvus) for semantic search and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).
IS 471 Cyber and AI Ethics — Responsible AI, bias mitigation, adversarial resilience, and governance frameworks aligned with NSA CyberAI standards.
IT 434 Cloud Computing — AWS/Azure ML pipelines, containerization (Docker/Kubernetes), and scalable deployment patterns.

Depth of Study: Artificial Intelligence (60 Credits)

Upper-division courses focusing on advanced AI systems, agentic architecture, and production deployment.

AI 410 AI Engineering — System design patterns, model serving, A/B testing frameworks, and production ML pipelines.
AI 420 Agentic Systems — Building autonomous agents: planning, reasoning loops, tool integration, and LLM orchestration.
AI 430 MLOps & AgentOps — Model monitoring, versioning, retraining pipelines, and operational excellence for agentic systems in production.
AI 440 Physical AI & Digital Twins — Integrating AI with robotics, IoT devices, and industrial systems. Simulation, control theory, and real-world deployment.
DS 479 Data Mining & Deep Learning — Deep neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, Transformers, and modern architectures. Backpropagation theory and practical implementation.

Strategic Constraints & Design Goals

1. The Math Gap Resolution

Traditional computer science curricula often leave students unprepared for Deep Learning mathematics. BSAI addresses this by embedding mathematical foundations directly into CS 251.

The Approach: We teach Linear Algebra and Calculus entirely through Tensor manipulation in Python. Students work with NumPy arrays as the primary vehicle, ensuring they understand:
  • Matrix operations and eigenvalue decomposition
  • Tensor broadcasting and reshaping for deep learning
  • GPU acceleration (CUDA fundamentals)
  • Automatic differentiation for backpropagation

2. Operational Technology (OT) Integration

Unlike standard CS degrees focused on web/cloud, BSAI specifically targets the intersection of AI and OT—a high-growth "blue ocean" market.

Differentiator: Coursework in Physical AI and Digital Twins prepares graduates for high-stakes roles in securing and managing industrial robotics, smart infrastructure, and critical systems—areas where traditional CS graduates lack expertise.

3. NSA/CyberAI Validation Alignment

The program explicitly targets the NSA SecureAI pathway and NCAE-C validation standards. This requires demonstrable rigor in:

Validation Requirement Supporting Courses Evidence Type
Adversarial Learning & Attack Vectors AI 440, IS 471, CS 469 Lab reports on adversarial robustness testing
Securing the AI Lifecycle AI 410, AI 430, IS 345 Model card documentation, security audits, threat assessments
Advanced Math for AI CS 251, DS 479 Mathematical proofs, optimization convergence analysis
AI Governance & Ethics IS 471 Policy documentation, stakeholder impact assessments

4. Market-Driven Hireability

The curriculum is explicitly designed to make graduates competitive at:

  • AI Labs: OpenAI, Meta AI Research, DeepMind, Anthropic — looking for engineers who can optimize and deploy cutting-edge models
  • Industrial AI: Boston Dynamics, Tesla Autopilot, Siemens Digital Industries — requiring Physical AI and OT expertise
  • Defense & Intelligence: NSA, DoD contractors — prioritizing CyberAI validation and secure AI systems
  • Cloud AI Platforms: AWS, Azure, Google Cloud — requiring cloud-native MLOps skills

Design Tasks & Validation Deliverables

1. Refine Course Syllabi

Objective: Ensure the "Math Bridge" in CS 251 creates a logical progression into Deep Learning and Physical AI labs.

  • CS 251 → DS 479: Linear Algebra via Tensors must directly enable backpropagation without additional remediation
  • CS 251 → AI 440: Vector calculus and optimization techniques must transfer to control systems and real-time learning
  • Deliverable: Detailed learning outcomes maps showing prerequisite mastery

2. Cross-Program Alignment

Objective: Position undergraduate core courses as a "Force Multiplier" for graduate programs (MS in Cyber, Data Science, and CS).

  • Articulation Pathways: Define how BSAI courses compress prerequisites for graduate study
  • Dual Enrollment: Identify which BSAI 400-level courses can count toward MS programs
  • Delivery: Articulation agreements with graduate programs, cross-listed courses

3. NSA CyberAI Validation Evidence

Objective: Verify that the program produces measurable student work products for validation audits.

  • Student Artifacts Required:
    • Lab reports demonstrating adversarial attack and defense methodologies
    • Model documentation following NSA guidelines
    • Mathematical proofs of algorithm correctness
    • Secure code audits and vulnerability assessments
    • AI governance and impact assessment documents
  • Validation Checkpoint: Annual review of student artifacts by NSA assessors
