BSAI 2026: Strategic Math & Course Roadmap

This proposal outlines a Python-first trajectory that bridges the mathematical foundations required for 2026 AI Engineering. By embedding "Computational Math" into the core engineering track, we eliminate structural gaps and prepare students for autonomous systems roles.

Roadmap 1: The Phased Course Sequence

Phase 1: Foundation (Year 1-2)
  • CS 132: Comp Science I (Java Logic)
  • IS 201: Fundamentals (Python Bridge)
  • CS 251: Stat Computing (Python Math)
  • MATH 141: Precalculus
Phase 2: The Core Engine (Year 3)
  • CS 469: Data Structures & Algorithms
  • AI 320: Fundamentals of AI
  • AI 370: Generative AI
  • IS 360: Database Technologies
Phase 3: Intelligence Layer (Year 3-4)
  • DS 479: Deep Learning
  • AI 430: AI Engineering
  • IT 434: Cloud Computing
  • IS 471: Cyber & AI Ethics
Phase 4: Autonomy (Year 4)
  • AI 410: MLOps & AgentOps
  • AI 420: Agentic Systems
  • AI 440: Physical AI & Digital Twins
  • CS 497: Capstone

Roadmap 2: Topic Mastery (The Math-to-AI Bridge)

Domain Core AI Topics Course Application
Linear Algebra Tensors, Matrix Decomposition (SVD), Eigenvectors CS 251 & CS 469: Engine for Semantic Retrieval & PCA
Calculus Partial Derivatives, Gradients, Chain Rule DS 479: Backpropagation in Neural Networks
Discrete Math Graph Theory, Logic, Complexity (Big O) CS 469 & AI 420: Orchestration of Autonomous Agents
Statistics Probability Distributions, Bayes' Theorem, CLT CS 251 & AI 320: Handling Sensor Noise in Physical AI

Meeting Strategy: Addressing Survival

To ensure Cybersecurity (CY) and Data Science (DS) students survive this shift, we frame these core requirements as "Computational Tool-Building":

  • For MS CY: Mastery of Data Structures and Algorithms logic is required to audit AI-driven security threats.
  • For MS DS: Shifting to Python-first Tensors in Statistical Computing removes the technical friction in Deep Learning.
  • For MS CS: The "Physical AI" track justifies the Operating Systems and Networking core as essential for Edge computing.
