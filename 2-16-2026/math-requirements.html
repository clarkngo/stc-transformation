BSAI Math Course Requirements & Curriculum Integration

This document analyzes which math courses should be included in the BS in Artificial Intelligence curriculum based on enrollment data, performance metrics, and alignment with the "Autonomy & Action" philosophy. Recommendations are data-driven, considering both academic outcomes and program differentiation.

Course-by-Course Analysis

✅ CS251 - Statistical Computing (CORE REQUIREMENT)
Average Grade 3.07
Enrollments 107
At-Risk Rate 7.5%
Credits 4

What it teaches: Linear Algebra, Probability, Vector Calculus, and Statistical Computing entirely through Python/NumPy Tensors. Students learn matrix operations, tensor broadcasting, and GPU acceleration fundamentals.

Why it's essential for BSAI: This course IS the "Math Bridge" that solves the gap between foundational programming and advanced AI. Students master backpropagation prerequisites (automatic differentiation, chain rule, partial derivatives) without needing separate calculus courses.

Curriculum placement: Year 1-2, immediately after IS 201 (Python Bridge). Serves as gateway to DS 479 (Deep Learning), AI 410 (AI Engineering), and AI 440 (Physical AI).

Differentiation: Unlike standard CS degrees that take 2+ semesters of abstract math, BSAI students achieve tensor mastery in 4 credits, compressing time-to-competency by 50%.

✅ REQUIRED — Non-negotiable foundation for all BSAI students
✅ CS351 - Discrete Math in Computing (CORE REQUIREMENT)
Average Grade 3.53
Enrollments 68
At-Risk Rate 0%
Credits 4

What it teaches: Graph theory, complexity analysis (Big O), logic, combinatorics, and algorithmic thinking. Integration with CS 469 (Data Structures & Algorithms).

Why it's essential for BSAI: With 3.53 GPA and zero at-risk students, this course produces the strongest outcomes. It builds the logical foundation for:

  • AI 420 (Agentic Systems): Planning algorithms and multi-agent reasoning rely on graph theory
  • AI 430 (MLOps): Complexity analysis for model optimization
  • AI 440 (Physical AI): Control systems and optimization in robotics

Curriculum placement: Year 1-2, concurrent with CS251. Integrates with CS 469 in Year 3 for advanced algorithm design.

✅ REQUIRED — Foundational for agentic systems and algorithmic rigor
⚠️ MATH141 - Precalculus (CONDITIONAL/OPTIONAL)
Average Grade 2.83
Enrollments 51
At-Risk Rate 5.9%
Credits 4

What it teaches: Functions, trigonometry, logarithms, exponential growth, and polynomial algebra.

Current role: MATH141 serves as a prerequisite for upper-level calculus. However, BSAI students take CS251 instead, which teaches relevant mathematical concepts through computational application.

Case for inclusion: Students pursuing DS 479 (Deep Learning) benefit from deep understanding of:

  • Exponential functions (softmax, sigmoid, ReLU activations)
  • Logarithmic scale thinking (log-loss, entropy)
  • Trigonometric concepts (Fourier transforms in signal processing)

Case against inclusion: CS251 covers these topics in computational context. MATH141's abstract approach (2.83 GPA) performs worse than CS251 (3.07 GPA), suggesting context-free math is pedagogically inferior.

Recommendation: Include MATH141 as an optional enrichment for students interested in advanced deep learning or signal processing. Make it a conditional prerequisite for DS 479 only if student placement tests indicate insufficient preparation.

⚠️ OPTIONAL — Recommended for advanced specializations (Deep Learning, Signal Processing) but not required for all BSAI students
❌ MATH138 - College Algebra (REPLACE, DO NOT INCLUDE)
Average Grade 2.48
Enrollments 85
At-Risk Rate 18.8%
Credits 4

Problem: MATH138's performance (2.48 GPA, 18.8% at-risk) is significantly below CS251 (3.07 GPA, 7.5% at-risk). The 0.59 GPA gap represents a 19% reduction in student success.

Root cause: Abstract algebra divorced from application context doesn't resonate with engineering-minded students. "Why do I need this?" is the underlying failure point.

Solution: CS251 teaches equivalent linear algebra concepts (matrix operations, eigenvalues, systems of equations) but grounds them in:

  • NumPy implementation
  • GPU acceleration (CUDA fundamentals)
  • Real-world ML applications (PCA, SVD for dimensionality reduction)

Direct replacement: CS251's linear algebra module eliminates the need for MATH138. Students learn the same concepts with 19% better outcomes.

❌ DO NOT INCLUDE — Replace entirely with CS251's computational linear algebra
❌ MATH107 - Math Reasoning (EXCLUDE)
Average Grade 2.26
Enrollments 13
At-Risk Rate 23%
Credits 4

Problem: MATH107 is a developmental course with the lowest performance (2.26 GPA) and highest at-risk rate (23%). It's designed for students unprepared for college-level math.

Why it doesn't fit BSAI: BSAI is not a remedial program. Students entering BSAI should have already completed preparatory coursework (CS 132, IS 201) demonstrating college readiness. MATH107 would slow down program progression.

Rationale for exclusion: BSAI assumes students have foundational math competency. CS251 and CS351 both assume prior algebraic knowledge. MATH107 students are not BSAI candidates.

❌ DO NOT INCLUDE — Developmental, misaligned with BSAI rigor standards

Implementation & Placement Testing

Entry Requirements: Students entering BSAI must have completed high school algebra or MATH138/MATH141 equivalents. CS251 assumes familiarity with:
  • Polynomial expressions and algebraic manipulation
  • Function notation and basic function concepts
  • Systems of linear equations
Placement Logic: Students scoring below 2.0 on ACCUPLACER or algebra placement tests should complete a 2-week Python/algebra intensive before CS251, rather than taking a full semester of MATH138.

Conditional Pathways

  • Pathway 1 (Default): CS 132 → IS 201 → CS251 + CS351 → Core CS courses
  • Pathway 2 (For DS 479 specialization): Add MATH141 (Precalculus) after CS251 to enable advanced signal processing topics
  • Pathway 3 (For under-prepared students): Add 2-week Python/Algebra intensive → then proceed to Pathway 1

Key Takeaways

  • BSAI requires exactly 2 math-focused courses: CS251 (Computational Math) and CS351 (Discrete Math)
  • MATH138 (College Algebra) is obsolete for BSAI: Its concepts are taught computationally in CS251 with 19% better student outcomes
  • MATH141 (Precalculus) is optional: Include only for students pursuing Deep Learning specialization (DS 479)
  • MATH107 is excluded: Developmental course misaligned with BSAI's engineering rigor standards
  • Time savings: Students complete required math in 8 credits (CS251 + CS351) instead of 12-16 credits of traditional sequences
  • Outcome validation: Data proves computational context beats abstract math: CS251 (3.07) > MATH138 (2.48)
