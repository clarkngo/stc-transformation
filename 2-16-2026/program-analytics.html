Key Findings & BSAI Implications

1. CS251 Success Story: With an average of 3.07 across 107 students and only 8 at-risk (7.5%), CS251's Python-first, computational mathematics approach is demonstrably effective. This validates the core design principle of the BSAI curriculum.
2. Discrete Math Mastery: CS351's exceptional performance (3.53 GPA, zero at-risk students) indicates that students who master algorithmic thinking excel in subsequent courses. This supports BSAI's emphasis on CS 469 (Data Structures & Algorithms) as a core requirement.
3. Program-Specific Patterns: Data Science students show the highest overall GPA (3.17), followed by Applied Computer Science (2.94). Cybersecurity students (2.88) still perform well in CS-specific courses but struggle more with pure math. This suggests BSAI's integrated "Computational Math" approach (CS251) is more effective than standalone algebra.
BSAI Design Validation: The data supports moving away from R-based statistics and pure abstract algebra toward Python-first, computationally-grounded mathematics. Students succeed when math is contextual and immediately applicable to coding and algorithms.

Recommendations for BSAI Curriculum & Recruitment

  • Target High-Performers: BSDS (3.17 GPA) and BSAPC (3.22 GPA) students are ideal candidates for BSAI specialization. Design targeted pathways for these cohorts.
  • Python-First Success: CS251's strong performance validates the Python-centric approach. Ensure BSAI emphasizes computational mathematics from day one.
  • Support Cybersecurity Integration: With 118 BSCY students and a dedicated CyberAI validation pathway, BSAI can serve as a force multiplier for cybersecurity specializations.
  • Address Math Gap: MATH138's lower performance (2.48 GPA) indicates students struggle with abstract algebra. BSAI's approach of embedding math in context (CS251) is the right solution.
  • Capacity Planning: With 256+ students in BSACS and BSCY, demand for BSAI specialization is substantial. Plan course sections accordingly.
