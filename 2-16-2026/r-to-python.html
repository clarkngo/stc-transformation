Curriculum Change Proposal: BS in AI

TO: Leadership Committee / Dean of Technology

FROM: AI Program Strategy Task Force

DATE: February 16, 2026

SUBJECT: Transitioning CS 251 to Python-First Engineering & Core Realignment

Executive Summary

To align with the 2026 AI Engineer Roadmap, we propose transitioning CS 251 (Statistical Computing) from R to a Python-centric "Computational Mathematics" framework. This shift addresses the "Math Gap" by bridging theoretical linear algebra with production-level engineering. Furthermore, the realignment from Generative AI to Machine Learning & Data Mining ensures our graduates possess the foundational rigor required for high-stakes roles in Cybersecurity and Operational Technology (OT).

The Rationale: Why Python over R?

While R is excellent for statistical visualization, the 2026 industry standard for MLOps and Agentic Systems is exclusively Python-based. Teaching math in Python allows students to master Tensors—the primary data structure for Neural Networks—months before they reach upper-division labs.

Pros of Python-First:
  • Seamless transition to DS 479 (Deep Learning).
  • Industry alignment (OpenAI, Meta, DeepMind).
  • Focus on GPU-accelerated "Vectorized" math.
Cons of Dropping R:
  • Minor loss of native statistical intuition.
  • Steeper initial coding curve for non-CS students.

Proposed Realignment (BSAI vs. CY MS DS)

Current Requirement Proposed Adjustment Strategic Value
Generative AI (AI 370) Machine Learning & Data Mining Builds foundational rigor before specialization.
CS 251 (R-Based) CS 251 (Python-Based) Eliminates the "Syntax Wall" between Prep and Core.
General Ethics Cyber & AI Ethics (IS 471) Prepares students for EU AI Act & global compliance.

Impact on Cybersecurity & OT

For Cybersecurity (CY) students, this shift is critical. Operational Technology (OT) security requires AI that can process high-dimensional sensor data in real-time. By mastering Python and Data Mining early, CY graduates can build Autonomous Agents capable of zero-day threat detection and MLOps pipeline management.

"AI Engineering is not about memorizing algorithms; it is about problem-solving, judgment, and picking the right tool at the right time." — 2026 Roadmap Mindset.
