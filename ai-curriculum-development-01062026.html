Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence (BSAI)

Curriculum Modernization Proposal | Date: January 6, 2026

1. Goal of the Proposal

The primary goal is to establish a world-class Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence that bridges the gap between academic research and industry application. By shifting the pedagogical focus from manual code production to AI System Architecture, we aim to produce graduates who can lead in the age of Agentic AI, leveraging "Vibe Coding" and multi-agent orchestration to solve enterprise challenges.

2. Summary of the Proposal

This proposal introduces a 50/50 Hybrid Curriculum Model. We optimize existing institutional resources by reusing foundational Data Science and CS courses while introducing high-impact, specialized AI modules. Key features include:

  • Top-Down Innovation: Research from the DIT program cascades into the BSAI Depth of Study.
  • Agentic Focus: Integration of the Model Context Protocol (MCP) and Google ADK as core technical competencies.
  • Modern SDLC: Transitioning from "software engineer" to "system architect" using AI-assisted development (Vibe Coding).

4. Strategic Reasoning

Operational Efficiency

The reuse of 50% of the curriculum reduces development overhead and ensures that foundational technical skills—database management and Python logic—are taught using proven, high-standard existing materials from the Data Science department.

Alignment with Tier-1 Institutions

Our focus on Agentic AI and Model Context Protocol (MCP) aligns with 2026 trends observed at Stanford and Berkeley, where "post-deployment" monitoring and multi-agent communication are now central to the AI software engineering lifecycle.

The Cascading Benefit

By aligning the BSAI Depth of Study with the DIT (Doctor of IT) framework, we create a clear academic pathway for students, ensuring that even undergraduate coursework is informed by doctoral-level research in Smart and Secure Systems.
