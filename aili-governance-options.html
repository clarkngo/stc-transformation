Why This Needs Deciding

AILI has a name and a mission — it doesn't yet have a home
What AILI is
The AI Lifelong Learning Institute — the non-degree layer covering the Maritime OT Security certificate, SPD/first-responder AI-literacy workshops, and the on-ramp that stacks into the BSAI degree. Leadership asked for a name that avoids "Center."
The constraint
AILI must complement, not disrupt, STC's core academic business — it's a testing ground alongside the degree programs, not a replacement structure.
The open question
The Dean of Education & Leadership already asked: "Does AILI sit under our school, or under STC?" She sees AILI as adjacent to her school's adult-learning expertise and wants a role in instructional design — not STC building it alone.
Precedent to reuse carefully: the Applied Intelligence Center (AIC) already has a worked org chart — one liaison, single-school, consulting-revenue shaped. AILI's problem is different: two schools have a real claim, and the revenue model is workshops/certificates, not consulting. Copying the AIC shape verbatim would answer the Dean's question by ignoring it.

Working Recommendation

A starting point for the workshop, not a decided position
Lean toward Option A. It's the only shape that resolves the tension already surfaced in our own meeting notes rather than moving it — neither Dean can say "the other school owns this." It's slower to charter, but that cost is mostly front-loaded (getting the President's Office to sponsor it), not recurring. B is the fastest path but reopens the exact objection the Education Dean raised; C fixes the pedagogy framing but creates a new objection from STC on the degree-pathway thread.

Open Questions for Tomorrow's Workshop

What we don't have visibility into yet — needs planning-team input
  • 1
    Where does the budget line actually sit right now — President's Office, STC, or Education & Leadership? This may constrain the option regardless of preference.
  • 2
    How has leadership been leaning verbally, beyond "avoid the word Center"? Any signal on single-school vs. cross-school ownership?
  • 3
    If Option A: who plays Director vs. the two Liaisons in Year 1 — internal hire, or split faculty release time from both schools?
  • 4
    Does the Maritime/SPD track need its own governance seam (external partners, grant funding) separate from the AI-degree/BSAI feeder track — i.e., is this really one org chart or two?
  • 5
    Timeline: does a charter decision need to land before the next President's Office touchpoint, or can this stay a working draft through one more cycle?
