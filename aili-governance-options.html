Why This Needed Deciding

AILI had a name and a mission — it didn't yet have a home
What AILI is
The AI Lifelong Learning Institute — CityU's continuing-education and non-degree layer, covering the Maritime OT Security certificate, The Frontline Professional seminar series for first responders, and the on-ramp that stacks into the BSAI degree. Leadership asked for a name that avoids "Center."
Scope
AILI is not limited to STC and Education & Leadership — it's built to serve any CityU school. Those two are the founding partners because they're first in line, not because they're the boundary; the Track Lead model is designed so another school can plug in later without a fresh governance decision.
The constraint
AILI must complement, not disrupt, STC's core academic business — it's a testing ground alongside the degree programs, not a replacement structure.
Resolved
The Dean of Education & Leadership had asked: "Does AILI sit under our school, or under STC?" Resolved by choosing Option A — AILI reports to the President's Office, not the Provost and not either school. Both Deans get a Liaison seat instead of ownership.
Earlier draft, same lineage — not a separate real thing: "Applied Intelligence Center" (AIC) was an earlier brainstormed name and org shape for this same institutional question — never established or approved. It evolved into AILI once the framing broadened past consulting into cross-school lifelong learning. The AIC draft's org chart (one liaison, single-school, consulting-revenue shaped) is useful as a worked example of the shape we're moving away from — not a standing structure AILI needs to coexist with or defer to.

Decision — Option A Confirmed

AILI reports to the President's Office — the Provost is out of the chain
Option A, President's Office only. It's the only shape that resolves the tension already surfaced in our own meeting notes rather than moving it — neither Dean can say "the other school owns this." The reporting line is the President's Office directly, not "Provost/President's Office" — a deliberate simplification so there's one accountable sponsor instead of two. B and C stay documented above for the record, but aren't the direction moving forward.

How Schools Connect to AILI

STC and Education & Leadership are the founding partners — the same pattern extends to any CityU school
Scope is cross-school, not two-school. STC and Education & Leadership are first because they're first in line — the Maritime and Frontline Professional tracks need STC's technical depth, and every non-degree track needs Education & Leadership's adult-pedagogy review. Any other CityU school that wants AILI's infrastructure or seminar-delivery capability plugs in the same way, billed through the internal service-fee model below.
STC contributes
  • Technical faculty fellows (AI, cybersecurity, data science) via release time
  • BSAI degree pathway & credentialing infrastructure for stacking
  • Open edX / GitHub delivery infrastructure already live in production
  • Domain research feeding the Maritime OT and AI-literacy curriculum
Education & Leadership contributes
  • Adult-learning pedagogy design — flipped Bloom's, competency-based model
  • Instructional design review for every seminar and certificate
  • Training for practitioner-instructors who aren't classroom-trained educators
  • Assessment and competency-framework standards
AILI delivers back to both
  • Real-world case studies (SPD pilot, Maritime OT engagements) for curriculum currency
  • Non-degree learner pipeline that can stack into BSAI or other CityU degrees
  • Seminar and certificate revenue share
  • External partner relationships (Port, SPD, sports orgs) both schools can draw on
  • Learner data passed to the marketing team for enrollment-funnel insight
The boundary that matters most: the AILI Director does not have unilateral authority over STC or Education & Leadership faculty time. All faculty and instructor assignments go through the relevant Liaison — this is what keeps the "complement, don't disrupt" promise real instead of aspirational.

Scope, Content & Licensing Model

Who can contribute, what tooling is used, and who owns what
Who can contribute
Not limited to CityU faculty. Any CityU school, any outside practitioner with an idea worth teaching, and partner nonprofits can develop and pitch course content through the same Track Lead pipeline.
Delivery & assessment tooling
Open edX stays the core platform. Capstone projects are evaluated with an AI tool sourced from a vendor — vendor selection is still open (see Next Steps).
Content ownership — open by default
Nobody owns AILI course content individually — it's open-source. Authors declare originality when they submit it; anyone downstream can reuse it as long as they give attribution back to the original author. The specific license (likely a Creative Commons Attribution variant) still needs to be selected — flagged in Next Steps.
Compliance exception
Tracks with a genuine compliance need (e.g. law-enforcement or regulatory content) can declare ownership instead of releasing under the open license — the open-by-default rule isn't absolute where it would create legal exposure.
Internal service-fee model
When another CityU school wants AILI's training, seminar delivery, or infrastructure, it's billed as an internal service fee — not a grant or a favor. This is what lets AILI scale across schools without every request becoming a governance conversation.

  • Service-based — internal fee when another school draws on AILI capacity
To Support
  • Infrastructure (Open edX hosting, tooling)
  • Training for instructors
  • Training for authors
Naming: seminar, not workshop. The delivery format is branded a seminar going forward — "workshop" stays reserved for internal planning sessions like this one. The SPD and Maritime frontline tracks ship under one external brand, The Frontline Professional, so officers and port/maritime workers see a single, recognizable series rather than two disconnected offerings.
Top-of-funnel: YouTube to course. Short-form content on YouTube and social media is the entry point — anybody with an idea worth selling pitches a topic, it gets tested as a video first, and only the ones that land turn into a full seminar or course. This is the same TOFU → MOFU → BOFU logic as the CityUX Open Ecosystem funnel — AILI is a content source feeding it, not a separate funnel.

Open Questions & Next Steps

Some resolved by research this week — the rest still need a decision, not a search
Resolved this week (research)
  • ✓
    Content license: use Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY). It matches the "nobody owns it, declare originality, attribution required" model exactly — permits reuse and commercial use, requires credit back to the author. "An advanced version of MIT for cloud" was likely a reference to AGPL, which is real but solves a different problem (it closes the SaaS "cloud loophole" for software licensing) — it doesn't apply to course content.
  • ✓
    Digital badges: the blocker is instance-admin access, not course-author permissions. Open edX badges are off by default — enabling them requires BADGES_ENABLED = True on both the LMS and Credentials services plus a connected Credly organization. Needs someone with instance-admin rights, not more access on Clark's account.
  • ✓
    Partner subdomains don't need a separate ~$78/mo instance each. Open edX supports true multi-tenancy on one instance via the eox-tenant plugin — multiple partner domains (e.g. a company's own subdomain) can run off the same backend with separate branding.
  • ✓
    WooCommerce integration already exists — worth reconsidering the "Year 2+" timing. A maintained plugin (openedx-wordpress-ecommerce) already syncs WooCommerce purchases to Open edX enrollment. This isn't a from-scratch build, so it may not need to wait as long as the phasing currently assumes.
Still open — needs a decision, not more research
  • 1
    Where does the budget line actually sit — fully inside the President's Office, or split with contributions from STC/Education & Leadership? Needed to finalize the charter.
  • 2
    Who plays Director vs. the two Liaisons in Year 1 — internal hire, or split faculty release time from STC and Education & Leadership?
  • 3
    Which vendor supplies the AI capstone-evaluation tool, and what does it cost per cohort? Shortlist to bake off: Gradescope (most established in higher ed), Disco, Coursebox, CoGrader, Crowdmark, Akindi.
  • 4
    What's the internal service-fee schedule when another CityU school draws on AILI capacity — flat rate, per-cohort, or negotiated per engagement?
  • 5
    Who approves a track's request to declare compliance-driven ownership instead of the default open license?
  • 6
    CE/license-renewal recognition needs profession-by-profession research. IACET accreditation covers general CEUs (legally recognized entity, ≥1 year in business, documented QA process, participant transcripts on file) — but it does not automatically satisfy profession-specific renewal (nursing boards, state bar CLE, CPA boards, engineering PE boards, real estate commissions each have their own separate approval process).
