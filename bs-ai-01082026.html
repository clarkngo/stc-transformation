BS in Artificial Intelligence (BSAI) Proposal

Proposed Date: January 6, 2026

Prepared by: Clark Ngo & Sam

1. Goal of the Proposal

The primary goal is to establish a high-impact Depth of Study that bridges the gap between academic data science and industry-level Agentic Engineering. This curriculum is designed to move students from traditional software development into System Architecture roles, focusing on the orchestration of autonomous AI agents that solve complex, real-world enterprise problems.

2. Summary of the Proposal

This 2026 curriculum update utilizes a 50/50 Hybrid Model to optimize university resources while maximizing student employability. By reusing core data infrastructure courses (DS 479, DS 484) and introducing three cutting-edge AI Agent modules (AI 475, 440, 450), the program provides a comprehensive "Full-Stack AI" path. The sequence concludes with a specialized elective (CS 453 or CS 454), allowing students to prove their skills in either Web or Mobile AI-powered environments.

4. Reasoning & Job Market Relevance

By 2026, the job market has shifted from "knowing how to code" to "knowing how to build AI-native systems".

  • AI 475 (Generative AI): HIGH DEMAND Provides the baseline expertise in Transformers required for any AI-related role. In the current market, "Prompt Engineering" has evolved into a deep technical requirement for all Software Engineers.
  • AI 440 & AI 450 (Agentic AI): CRITICAL SKILL Companies are no longer hiring for simple chatbots; they are building Agentic Workforces. Mastery of the Model Context Protocol (MCP) allows students to create agents that actually "do things"—interacting with CRMs, databases, and external APIs.
  • DS 479 & DS 484 (Data Foundation): Reusing these courses ensures graduates understand the "fuel" for AI. Modern agents are only as good as the Big Data pipelines feeding them. This foundation prevents "hallucinations" and ensures statistical rigor.
  • CS 453/454 (Applied Choice): This is the "Last Mile" of development. The ability to embed a multi-agent AI system (AI 440/450) into a secure, production-ready Web or Mobile app is exactly what enterprises are looking for to complete their Digital Transformation.

6. Comparative Program Alignment

Our program aligns with the 2026 trends from Stanford and CMU, where "Seniority" is defined by the ability to steer AI effectively rather than manual coding speed. By incorporating MCP and Agentic SDLC, our BSAI students will be trained on the same protocols currently being researched at Berkeley for building "Safe and Trustworthy AI Agents."

This proposal follows the Cascading Model: Higher-level DIT research (Smart Systems) informs the MS and BS AI depth of study.
