Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence (BSAI)

Program Proposal: Transitioning from Manual Coding to Agentic Orchestration

Why BSAI? The Strategic Advantage

The BSAI is not just a degree in "smarter coding." It is a foundational shift in how humans solve problems across the entire global economy. By 2026, the value of a tech professional has moved from knowing how to write code to knowing how to orchestrate intelligence.

AI as a Domain-Agnostic Catalyst

This program emphasizes that AI is no longer a technical silo, but a catalyst that multiplies human capability across every professional domain:

Healthcare

Using predictive analytics and "ambient intelligence" to reduce clinician burnout and improve diagnostics.

Finance & Trust

Designing transparent, fair AI tools for real-time risk assessment and hyper-personalized banking.

Sustainability

Optimizing resource management, energy grids, and predictive agriculture through Digital Twins.

Job Prospects Beyond Engineering

Graduates are prepared for high-impact careers as:

  • AI Solutions Architect: Designing the "brain" of a company's data ecosystem.
  • Agentic Ops Manager: Monitoring and scaling fleets of autonomous agents in manufacturing or IT.
  • AI Ethics Lead: Ensuring algorithmic accountability and privacy compliance.
  • Bioinformatics Analyst: Using AI to accelerate drug discovery and clinical diagnostics.

The "Spec-First" Pedagogical Philosophy

In a world where AI can generate boilerplate syntax, the human premium shifts to high-level design, experimental interpretation, and ethical oversight. Traditionally, students spend 80% of their time on syntax; in the BSAI, they spend 80% on Architectural Logic.

# SPECIFICATION: AGENTIC_CAREER_COACH
TYPE: Autonomous Agent Workflow
INTENT: Analyze LinkedIn profiles and generate personalized upskilling roadmaps.
STATE: Persistent Context via Vector Database.
GUARDRAILS: Human-in-the-Loop approval required for external emails.

The Specification is the source of truth. Students learn to "Pilot" agents (like Google Antigravity or Claude) to execute these specs, focusing on refining the logic and building "backstops" to ensure reliability.

Faculty Hearing: Advocate vs. Skeptic

Technology Skeptic Professor: "I am worried about foundational erosion. If AI generates the code, how do we know the student truly understands memory management or network packet flow? And frankly, we don't have the manpower to build 30 credits of new material from scratch."
AI Advocate Professor: "We aren't building from scratch. Our '50/50 Strategy' leverages 90 credits of our existing core. The AI doesn't replace the foundation; it acts as the instrument. In our 'Networking' course, students use agents to simulate packet loss across massive clusters—tasks they couldn't do manually. We are training Pilots, not just mechanics. The cognitive load isn't lower; it's shifted to the architectural level."
Technology Skeptic Professor: "And the integrity? If they submit a project built by an agent, how is that their own?"
AI Advocate Professor: "Every project requires a 'Pilot Log.' We grade the reasoning trace—the interventions, the discovery questions, and the logical audits. If the AI hallucinates and the student doesn't catch it, they fail. This forces a deeper level of engagement than traditional copy-paste coding ever did."

Anticipated Concerns (Stakeholder FAQ)

From the Advisement Team

How do I explain "Agentic Systems" to a non-tech prospective student?
Tell them it's the difference between being a mechanic and an aerospace engineer. Instead of just turning wrenches (coding syntax), they are designing the entire flight system (AI Orchestration). It's for people who want to lead and design.

From Content Development & SMEs

How do we keep course materials fresh when tools change monthly?
We focus modules on Principles of Orchestration (e.g., Spec writing, Debugging logic) rather than specific UI buttons. We use "living labs" where students are encouraged to use the current latest tools within our established architectural frameworks.
Does using AI weaken problem-solving skills?
No, it shifts the problem. Instead of solving a syntax typo, they are solving "logic drift" or ensuring an agent doesn't overstep its security bounds. The problem-solving is more complex and higher-stakes.

From the Program Director & Professors

I'm not an expert in "Agentic Systems" yet. How will I teach this?
The 50/50 Strategy means most faculty stay in their comfort zone (OS, Networking). For new AI courses, we provide "Co-Pilot Faculty Kits"—templated labs and AI-tutor bots to assist in the classroom.
How does this affect our accreditation?
By grounding 50% of the program in established CS standards (OS, Networks, Cloud), we maintain core competencies while the "Depth of Study" adds the innovation layer required for modern competitiveness.

From Students

Will this degree be valuable if AI starts writing 100% of the code?
Yes. The value is in being the person who decides what to build and verifies that it's safe. AI can write code, but it can't take responsibility for a business outcome—you can. You aren't competing with AI; you're the one in control of it.

Academic Integrity & "The Pilot Log"

The Principle of Transparent Orchestration: Students are permitted to use agents (Claude, Cursor, Lovable) provided they act as the primary architect.

  • Required: Submission of a "Pilot Log" documenting every intervention where the student corrected the agent.
  • Prohibited: "Unverified Delegation"—submitting AI output without personal validation of logic, security, and ethics.
