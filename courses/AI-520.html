AI 520: Natural Language Processing for Artificial Intelligence - Proposed Assessments

Course Description: Covers fundamental concepts and advanced techniques in Natural Language Processing (NLP), including text representation, language models, sentiment analysis, and transformer architectures.

1. Applied Case Studies / Conceptual Assessment (40%)

Example Scenario: Bias Detection in Online Reviews

Prompt: A popular local online review platform for restaurants has received complaints about potential gender or racial bias in its user-generated content. As an NLP specialist, design an approach to detect and quantify such biases. Prepare a technical report (5-page max) and a mock presentation (slides only) outlining:

  • The specific NLP techniques (e.g., sentiment analysis, topic modeling, word embeddings) you would use for detection.
  • How you would define and measure 'bias' computationally in this context, citing relevant research papers.
  • The ethical challenges of deploying such a system and proposed mitigation strategies.

Why AI-Resistant? Requires deep understanding of NLP techniques, critical thinking about complex ethical definitions (e.g., "bias"), and application to a specific, nuanced real-world problem. While AI can summarize NLP concepts, it cannot design a robust, ethical solution for a hypothetical, specific platform and defend its choices.

Example Viva Questions (Post-Submission)

  • "You proposed using [Specific NLP Technique]. Can you explain a scenario where its results might be misleading in detecting subtle bias, and how you would apply your **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** to validate the findings qualitatively?"
  • "How might an **AI Agent** assist you in drafting the initial technical report on bias detection, and what specific NLP-related details did you have to critically verify from its output?"
  • "Beyond detection, what are the ethical implications of *intervening* to correct bias in user-generated text, and what NLP methods could be used for such an intervention?"

2. Team Project: Custom Chatbot Development (40%)

Example Scenario: Community Support Chatbot

Prompt: Your team is tasked with developing a functional chatbot for a local non-profit (e.g., a food bank, a community health clinic) to answer frequently asked questions and direct users to appropriate resources. The chatbot should:

  • Utilize a chosen NLP framework (e.g., Rasa, NLTK, Hugging Face transformers).
  • Handle at least 5 distinct user intents (e.g., "what are your hours?", "how to volunteer?").
  • Integrate with a simple knowledge base (provided).

Your Project Portfolio must include the functional chatbot, detailed **architecture diagrams** explaining the NLP pipeline, the training data used, and individual reflections on challenges and **AI Agent** usage in development.

Why AI-Resistant? Requires the practical application of NLP principles to build a functional system for a specific domain. While AI can generate code snippets, building, training, debugging, and documenting a custom chatbot that handles specific intents and integrates with a knowledge base demands significant human effort and understanding.

Example Viva Questions (Post-Submission)

  • "Your chatbot used [Specific NLP Framework]. What was the most challenging aspect of training it to recognize a specific user intent, and how did your team apply **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** to improve its accuracy?"
  • "Explain the trade-offs your team considered between using a rule-based approach versus a machine learning approach for a particular intent."

3. Programming Exercises (PE) (20%)

Example Scenario: Fine-tuning a Pre-trained Language Model (LLM)

Prompt: Given a pre-trained small language model (e.g., a BERT variant from Hugging Face) and a specific, niche dataset (e.g., legal document classification, medical abstract summarization), fine-tune the model for a new task. Your task is to:

  • Implement the fine-tuning process.
  • Evaluate its performance using appropriate metrics.
  • Submit the code, performance metrics, and a **Prompt Engineering Log & Reflection** detailing your use of **AI Agents** to generate fine-tuning scripts, data preprocessing steps, and evaluation code. Include your critical evaluation of the AI's suggestions and any debugging you performed.

Why AI-Resistant? Focuses on practical NLP model adaptation, critical evaluation of AI-generated code for specific datasets, and debugging the intricate process of fine-tuning, which often requires significant human intervention and understanding of the model's behavior.

Example PE-Related Discussion Questions (Class Forum / Instructor Check-in)

  • "Describe a specific instance where an **AI Agent's** suggestion for data preprocessing led to a negative impact on your model's performance, and how your **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** helped you identify the problem."
  • "What prompt did you use to generate the initial fine-tuning script, and how did you refine it after the first attempt to improve performance?"
