CS 622: Discrete Math and Algorithms for Computing - Proposed Assessments

Course Description: Covers foundational discrete mathematics concepts (e.g., logic, set theory, graph theory) and advanced algorithm design and analysis, crucial for understanding complex AI and computing systems.

1. Applied Case Studies / Conceptual Assessment (40%)

Example Scenario: Resource Allocation in a Cloud AI Cluster

Prompt: An **AI in Cloud Computing (AI 510)** system manager needs to optimize the allocation of GPU resources for various **Deep Learning** models, each with different computational requirements and deadlines. Design a mathematical model (using graph theory or dynamic programming concepts) to minimize wait times or maximize GPU utilization. Prepare a formal technical paper (6-8 pages) and a proof-of-concept visual simulation (e.g., using Python Matplotlib or JavaScript). Your paper must:

  • Clearly define your objective function and constraints.
  • Propose an algorithm to solve the allocation problem, justifying its computational complexity (Big O notation).
  • Analyze the trade-offs of your approach under various load conditions.

Why AI-Resistant? Requires applying abstract mathematical/algorithmic concepts to a nuanced computing problem. While AI can explain algorithms, it struggles with generating novel, optimized solutions for complex, multi-constrained problems and deriving Big O analysis from first principles without explicit guidance. The visual simulation further enforces understanding.

Example Viva Questions (Post-Submission)

  • "You opted for Algorithm X. Explain a specific edge case where your chosen algorithm might perform poorly, and how a different algorithm (e.g., greedy vs. dynamic programming) might handle it differently."
  • "What specific **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** (e.g., using a debugger, profiling code) did you use to verify the correctness and efficiency of your implemented algorithm in the simulation?"
  • "How might an **AI Agent** assist you in exploring different algorithmic approaches, and what part of its output (e.g., proof steps) did you need to critically verify for mathematical rigor?"

2. Team Project: Algorithmic Solution for a Graph Problem (40%)

Example Scenario: Optimizing Delivery Routes for AI Agents

Prompt: Your team is developing an autonomous delivery system using multiple **AI Agents** (AI 610) that need to navigate a dynamic city network (represented as a graph with changing traffic weights). Design and implement an algorithm to find optimal paths or manage agent assignments under real-time constraints. Your **Project Portfolio** must include:

  • Phase 1: Problem Formalization & Algorithm Design: Clearly define the graph model, objective, and constraints. Design a specific algorithm (e.g., Dijkstra variant, A*, matching algorithm) with pseudocode and formal analysis.
  • Phase 2: Implementation & Testing: Implement the algorithm in a chosen language and test its performance on various graph sizes and complexities.
  • Phase 3: Scalability Analysis & Reflection: Analyze the algorithm's scalability for large-scale cities and reflect on the algorithmic trade-offs, individual contributions, and **AI Agent** usage in debugging/optimization.

Why AI-Resistant? Requires deep understanding of graph algorithms, their formal analysis, and practical implementation for a dynamic problem. AI can provide standard algorithm code, but adapting, optimizing, and formally analyzing a solution for a novel, dynamic constraint set (e.g., changing traffic weights) demands human expertise.

Example Viva Questions (Post-Submission)

  • "Your algorithm is a modification of [Standard Algorithm]. Explain how your modification addresses the specific 'dynamic traffic' constraint, and why a purely standard approach would fail."
  • "Given a specific graph structure, what is the tightest upper bound on the time complexity of your algorithm, and how would you prove it?"

3. Programming Exercises (PE) (20%)

Example Scenario: Formal Proof & Algorithm Implementation

Prompt: Given a problem related to set theory or combinatorics (e.g., counting unique permutations under specific constraints), your task is to:

  • Write a formal mathematical proof for a specific property of the problem.
  • Implement an algorithm to compute the solution.
  • Submit the proof, code, and a **Prompt Engineering Log & Reflection** detailing your use of **AI Agents** to explore proof strategies, generate code, and verify mathematical claims. Include your critical evaluation of the AI's output and any corrections you made.

Why AI-Resistant? Focuses on the student's ability to construct formal mathematical proofs (where AI often 'hallucinates' logical steps) and critically verify complex claims. The AI acts as a brainstorming partner, but the rigor and validation are human responsibilities.

Example PE-Related Discussion Questions (Class Forum / Instructor Check-in)

  • "Describe a moment when an **AI Agent's** mathematical proof or algorithmic suggestion contained a subtle error. How did your **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** in discrete math or debugging help you pinpoint and correct it?"
  • "What prompt did you find most effective for asking the AI to 'critique' its own generated proof or code for errors?"
