DS 510: Artificial Intelligence for Data Science - Proposed Assessments

Course Description: Explores how AI concepts and techniques are applied within the data science workflow, from data acquisition and preprocessing to model building, evaluation, and deployment.

1. Applied Case Studies / Conceptual Assessment (40%)

Example Scenario: Customer Churn Prediction for a Local ISP

Prompt: A small, local internet service provider (ISP) is experiencing unexpected customer churn. Using a provided anonymized dataset (specific to a hypothetical local ISP, e.g., 'San Diego FiberNet'), analyze customer behavior to predict churn. Prepare a data science report (8-10 pages) and a set of key insights slides for the ISP's marketing team. Your report must:

  • Detail your data exploration and feature engineering process.
  • Compare at least two **Machine Learning** models (e.g., Logistic Regression, Random Forest), justifying your choice of metrics and model selection.
  • Translate your findings into actionable recommendations for reducing churn, specifically tailored to the local ISP's challenges.

Why AI-Resistant? Requires applying data science principles to a specific, unique dataset with contextualized business recommendations. While AI can build models, the critical thinking for feature engineering on a novel dataset, justifying model choices with domain context, and generating *actionable, specific* business insights remains human-driven.

Example Viva Questions (Post-Submission)

  • "You engineered Feature X from the raw data. Explain the business intuition behind this feature's importance, and what specific **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** (e.g., SQL querying, Python data manipulation) you used to create it."
  • "Your chosen model achieved an accuracy of Y. What are its limitations regarding interpretability, and how might that affect the marketing team's trust in your recommendations?"
  • "How might an **AI Agent** assist you in drafting the initial data science report, and what specific analytical interpretations did you have to critically verify from its output?"

2. Team Project: End-to-End AI/ML Pipeline for Data Analysis (40%)

Example Scenario: Predictive Maintenance System

Prompt: Your team is tasked with building a full-stack, data-driven system to predict equipment failure (e.g., HVAC systems in campus buildings, local manufacturing line components) using sensor data. The system should:

  • Ingest streaming sensor data (simulated).
  • Train a **Machine Learning** model (e.g., time-series prediction) to detect anomalies or predict failures.
  • Provide a simple dashboard (e.g., using Streamlit, Dash) to visualize predictions and model confidence.

Your Project Portfolio must include the functional system, detailed **architecture diagrams** (showing data flow, model serving), comprehensive code, and individual reflections on challenges and **AI Agent** usage in development.

Why AI-Resistant? Requires integrating multiple components (data ingestion, ML model, visualization) into a cohesive system, which involves complex architectural decisions and debugging. While AI can provide code for individual components, the integration and overall system design require human oversight and problem-solving.

Example Viva Questions (Post-Submission)

  • "Your system uses [Specific ML Model] for predictive maintenance. Explain how your team managed potential data drift in the sensor readings, and what **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** were critical for monitoring data quality."
  • "If your model frequently produced false positives, what steps would your team take to debug the model, and how would you iterate on your **Prompt Engineering** to get better debugging insights from an **AI Agent**?"

3. Programming Exercises (PE) (20%)

Example Scenario: Advanced Feature Engineering & Data Preprocessing

Prompt: Given a raw, messy dataset (e.g., customer transaction logs, sensor readings with missing values and outliers), your task is to:

  • Perform comprehensive data cleaning, transformation, and advanced feature engineering using Python libraries.
  • Justify each step with statistical or domain-specific reasoning.
  • Submit the processed dataset, the Python script, and a **Prompt Engineering Log & Reflection** detailing your use of **AI Agents** to explore different preprocessing techniques, generate code for transformations, and identify potential issues in the raw data. Include your critical evaluation of the AI's suggestions.

Why AI-Resistant? Focuses on the often-messy, iterative process of real-world data preprocessing, where human judgment is paramount. While AI can suggest code, the student must apply **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** to detect subtle issues in the data and critically evaluate if AI's transformations are appropriate for the specific dataset and downstream **Machine Learning** task.

Example PE-Related Discussion Questions (Class Forum / Instructor Check-in)

  • "Share an instance where an **AI Agent's** suggestion for handling missing values in your dataset was inappropriate, and how your **Hands-On Skills (HOS)** helped you identify a more suitable method."
  • "What prompt did you use to ask the AI to suggest 'advanced' feature engineering techniques, and which ones did you find most effective for your dataset?"
