Dangerously Self-Educated:
The ACTOR Framework for STC

A plan for transforming STC's learning design from passive content consumption into outcome-driven knowledge formation — using the ACTOR framework as the organizing spine. Transformation, not completion, is the metric.

👤 Clark Ngo 🏫 City University of Seattle · STC 📅 June 2026 🎥 How To Become Dangerously Self-Educated (with AI)
Section 01 — The Core Insight

Transformation, Not Consumption

"Most people read books and forget them but the top 1% read to change something and to become dangerously smart."
On Purpose
"If you want to change the way you think you have to change the way you read."
On Mindset
"Reading was never just about books — it was about training for complexity: how you follow an argument, hold two opposing ideas in your head, change your mind when new evidence comes, and form an opinion that actually belongs to you."
On Complexity
"Most people read a book as tourists. The best readers read it as a spy."
On Active Learning

The top 1% of learners don't read books to finish them — they read to actively change how they think, decide, communicate, and perform. The same principle applies to education: the goal of any STC course is not completion of labs or assessments. It is measurable transformation in how students reason and act.

🎯

The central question every STC course must answer

Not: "Did the student complete the work?" — but: "How did this course change how they think, build, and decide?" If we cannot answer that question with evidence, the course is producing passive consumers, not professionals.

"The difference between a passive reader and a serious one is not speedreading anymore — it is your position. Are you sitting outside the book watching the author think, or are you becoming an actor in the story?"

Section 02 — The Traps We Must Break

Learning Myths That Are Actively Hurting STC Students

Before deploying the ACTOR framework, we must identify the false beliefs that make passive learning feel like real learning. Each myth has a direct STC manifestation.

❌ The Illusion of Fluency

Students mistake a well-explained lecture or lab for their own understanding. Confidence collapses the moment they are asked to explain the concept step-by-step. This is why STC oral defenses expose gaps that written submissions always hide.

❌ The AI Shortcut Myth

Using AI to generate a solution, summary, or README feels productive but prevents students from wrestling with core ideas. You cannot build mental "muscles" if you don't do the "push-ups" yourself. AI as shortcut = credential without competence.

❌ The Completion Trap

Finishing the lab, submitting the PE, closing the task — without letting any of it actually change you. STC's current HOS model runs entirely on the completion trap: students are rewarded for doing, not for transforming.

❌ The Highlighter Trap

Mistaking annotation and note-taking for remembering. Students who carefully document every lab step have often learned nothing they can retrieve. Highlighting is passive re-exposure, not encoding.

❌ The Summary Trap

Writing notes, READMEs, or reflections that are never read again — and never stress-tested under retrieval. Module 01 (AI Documentation) risks becoming a summary trap unless students are required to defend their README claims live.

❌ The Learning Styles Myth

Labeling yourself "visual" or "kinesthetic" creates a self-inflicted ceiling unsupported by science. STC students who say "I'm not a theory person" or "I'm not a coder" are using identity labels to avoid the discomfort of hard encoding. We must explicitly dismantle this.

"The label creates the ceiling."
The Video · On Learning Style Myths
"You're not going to grow your muscles if you haven't done a single push-up yourself."

Section 03 — The ACTOR Framework

Five Steps Where AI Is a Sidekick, Not a Shortcut

The ACTOR framework governs how STC students engage with every major learning task. At each step, AI plays a specific, bounded role — accelerating the human's thinking rather than replacing it. The framework works for reading, for project work, for lab design, and for self-directed upskilling.

A

Aim

Read / Learn with Purpose

Approach every learning task as a spy, not a tourist. Before starting a lab, a module, or a course, write a single mission statement: "I am doing this because I need to solve X." This turns passive consumption into active construction — you are hunting for specific information, not absorbing everything.

Without a clear aim, students complete work without transforming. The aim is the difference between a student who "did the lab" and one who used the lab to solve a real problem they identified.

"Your purpose turns any reading from consumption to construction. Without that one sentence the book will decide what matters — with your purpose, you decide what to hunt for."
The Video · Aim
AI Role: Framer
Tell AI what professional problem or bottleneck you are trying to solve. Ask it to generate three guiding questions to carry into the learning task. AI sharpens your aim — it does not set it.
STC Application
Every HOS sprint begins with a student-written Aim statement: one sentence identifying the professional bottleneck this lab unblocks. Instructor reviews Aim statements before the sprint begins. No aim = no credit.
C

Compress

Find the Core Idea (the Trunk)

Don't learn to collect more information — learn to carry less. Using Elon Musk's analogy: knowledge is a tree. Students must identify the trunk (the core, load-bearing principle) instead of collecting leaves (syntax, examples, anecdotes, steps).

A student who completes HOS 10A and can recite the RAG stack is collecting leaves. A student who can explain why spec-first design eliminates architectural drift in any stack has found the trunk.

"Don't read a book to collect more — read it to carry less. A book is not meant to be carried page by page; it has to be compressed into something your mind can hold."
The Video · Compress
AI Role: Interpreter
After engaging with the material, write your own compressed version of the core idea. Then ask AI: "Check my interpretation — what did I miss, overstate, or get wrong?" AI stress-tests your compression, not generate it.
STC Application
Module 01 (Documentation) redesigned: instead of an AI-generated README, students write a 2-sentence "trunk statement" in their own words first, then use AI as interpreter to pressure-test it. The trunk statement is what gets graded.
T

Test

Read / Build to Disagree

The best learners actively look for ideas to reject or challenge. Instead of discarding ideas that challenge existing beliefs, they treat disagreement as self-discovery: "What underlying assumption am I trying to protect?"

STC students who only build what they already know never encounter the friction where real learning happens. Disagreement is not a distraction — it is the mechanism.

"The best readers don't read to agree — they read to find what they want to reject. If a book only makes you comfortable, it's not going to change you."
The Video · Test
AI Role: Sparring Partner
Ask AI to challenge your architecture, find your hidden assumptions, and give its best counterarguments to your design decisions. This is the anti-pattern of "AI, write my code." This is AI as Socratic adversary.
STC Application
Module 06 (Socratic Tutor) fully embodies this step. Extend it: every major PE requires a student-submitted "Challenge Log" — three AI challenges the student prompted, and their written responses. The debate is the deliverable.
O

Own

Internalize Through Teaching

Rereading creates the illusion of familiarity. Looking away and recalling from memory is what builds durable knowledge. The ultimate test: if you cannot teach it to someone else, you do not own it yet.

Connect every technical concept to a real situation, a past mistake, or a future application. Abstract knowledge that is never anchored atrophies. The Guild's Journeyman teach-back requirement is the exact mechanism for this step.

"Meaning gives memory a place to live."  ·  "If you cannot teach it, you do not own it yet."  ·  "Buying a book means that you own the object — the hard part is to own what's inside it."
The Video · Own
AI Role: Coach
Ask AI to help you explain a concept in plain English or map it to a concrete business analogy. Then close the chat and explain it again without AI. If you can't, you haven't owned it yet — return to Compress.
STC Application
Oral defense gates every major deliverable. Retrieval under pressure — not submission — is the primary grade signal. Guild Journeymen are required to teach Phase I content to Apprentices as their succession deliverable.
R

Run

Turn Knowledge into Behavior Change

Knowledge only matters if it changes behavior in the real world. A leadership concept should change how you run your team. An architecture principle should change how you design your next system. Learning that produces no behavioral change is decoration.

This is the final test of STC education: not "what did you learn?" but "what do you do differently now?" Capstones, internship deliverables, and Guild projects are only valuable if they demonstrate this behavioral delta.

"Books have always been civilization software updates. So the question isn't whether a book changes the world — it can. The question is whether it changes you."
The Video · Run
AI Role: Action Companion
Ask AI to turn a concept into one concrete decision, a new rule, a checklist, or an experiment you will run this week. Not "explain this further" — "help me act on this."
STC Application
Every capstone portfolio requires a "Run Log": three specific decisions or behaviors the student changed as a direct result of their learning. This is the transformation evidence. No Run Log = incomplete portfolio.
Section 04 — The Real Competitive Edge

Access to Intelligence Is No Longer an Advantage

"In the age of AI everyone is going to have access to the same summaries. The edge is no longer your access to intelligence — the real edge is what you bring to it as a human being: your judgment, your taste, and a point of view that is uniquely yours."
The Video · On Human Judgment vs. AI

Because AI makes summaries, polished notes, and working code available to everyone, knowledge access has been commoditized. A student who can retrieve a fact faster than AI is irrelevant. A student who has unique judgment, taste, and a distinct point of view is irreplaceable.

🚫

What AI has commoditized

Recall, summaries, syntax, boilerplate, first drafts, data formatting, basic analysis, completion of guided tasks. Any STC assessment that grades these is grading something AI does for free.

✅

What remains irreplaceably human

Judgment under ambiguity, architectural taste, the ability to challenge a design, connecting concepts across disciplines, teaching others, and making decisions that reflect values — not just optimization.

💡

Deep reading as a mirror — and deep building as a reveal

The framework argues that serious learners become better at reading people, reading rooms, and reading silence — because deep engagement with ideas reveals your hidden assumptions and inherited biases. For STC: students who go through the ACTOR process rigorously do not just build better systems. They develop the meta-cognitive muscle to know when they understand something and when they are only performing understanding. That is the rarest and most valuable professional skill in the AI age.

"After a while the books you read start reading you — they help you reveal the stories that you tell yourself, the fears you protect, the assumptions that you've inherited."
The Video · On Self-Awareness Through Deep Learning
"The deeper you read, the better you start reading people — you read the room better, you read silence better. That's why serious leaders are serious readers."
The Video · On Leadership

Section 06 — Implementation Roadmap

Phased Rollout: FY 2026–2027

Summer 2026 · Weeks 1–6

Phase A: Faculty Run the ACTOR Framework on Themselves

Faculty identify one real professional bottleneck (curriculum design, AI upskilling, industry alignment) and work through all five ACTOR steps with AI as sidekick — not as assistant. The goal is first-hand experience of the transformation model before teaching it. Deliverable: one redesigned module brief using Aim-first structure.

Faculty Workshop Aim Statements AI Sparring Sessions
Fall 2026 · Pilot Cohort

Phase B: Pilot in AI 251 + HOS 10A

Deploy ACTOR in two courses with clear transformation checkpoints. AI 251 (preparatory bridge): every topic anchored to a student-identified aim. HOS 10A (spec-driven lab): full ACTOR cycle embedded in the project sprint. Track Aim statement quality, Challenge Log depth, and oral defense pass rates. Collect before/after behavioral change evidence.

AI 251 HOS 10A Oral Defense Gate Challenge Log Run Log
Spring 2027 · Full STC Rollout

Phase C: ACTOR Embedded in All STC Core Courses

Each course ships an ACTOR Map: what is the Aim of this course (what bottleneck does it unblock?), how Compress is tested (trunk statement rubric), how Test is enforced (Challenge Log requirements), how Own is gated (oral defense rubric), and what Run evidence is required (Run Log prompt). The AI Infusion 7-module framework is updated to embed ACTOR explicitly.

Summer 2027 · Guild Integration

Phase D: The Guild as a Living ACTOR System

Every Guild project archive includes the original Aim statement, the Compress artifacts, the Challenge Log, the Oral Defense recording summary, and the team's Run Log. Journeymen are required to teach at least one Compress → Own cycle to Apprentices as their succession deliverable. The Library becomes a retrieval-practice repository, not a passive archive. Section 08 — Why This, Why Now
🔑

The core competitive argument

Every other institution is trying to ban AI or catch students using it. STC's competitive advantage is building students who use AI better than anyone else — not as a shortcut, but as a structured sidekick at each ACTOR step. The student who uses AI as a sparring partner to stress-test their own thinking will outperform the student who uses it to generate answers every single time.

The ACTOR framework lands precisely because STC already has the infrastructure: the AI Infusion modules provide the content substrate, the Guild's succession model provides the Own + Run mechanism, the AI-Empowered Portfolio model provides the assessment structure, and spec-driven development provides the Aim-first design pattern.

What the framework provides is the organizing principle that ties every piece together — shifting the question from "did students complete the work?" to "how did this change them?"

That shift is not idealistic. It is the only defensible position in a world where AI can complete any assignment. The students who graduate from STC should be identifiable not by their transcripts but by the quality of their judgment, the discipline of their thinking, and the clarity of what they stand for. That is what it means to be dangerously self-educated.
