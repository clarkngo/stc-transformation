AILI Governance & Organizational Structure

Scope, placement, and how it shares resources with STC
Scope & placement
AILI is a non-degree initiative designed to handle agile projects from the President. It should sit directly under the Chief of Staff / President's Office rather than the Provost — the Provost's role is primarily degree-granting academic accreditation.
Resource sharing
AILI complements STC rather than disrupting it. Sam proposed a shared-resource approach — e.g. borrowing 50% or 25% of Clark's capacity for AILI while he keeps his academic ties to STC.
Worth reconciling on review: this summary frames the reporting line as "Chief of Staff / President's Office," while the governance page currently records the decision as President's Office only. The underlying transcript has Sam floating Chief of Staff as a live open question ("this organization may be placed under Chief of Staff, too... I don't know"), not a settled second option — so this is likely the same ambiguity restated, not a new decision. Flagging rather than resolving it here.

Open Source & IP Model

Licensing approach and the funnel mechanic it enables
Open-source framework
Courses and materials generally follow an open-source licensing model (e.g. MIT or a cloud-adapted MIT variant) to ensure broad distribution, attribution, and legal clarity.
Gated downloads & data capture
Free resources (like Clark's AI ebook) serve as top-of-funnel (TOFU) touchpoints. Users submit basic information — name, email, role — to download materials, generating marketing and prospect data for the university.
Already researched: the governance page's Open Questions section resolves the specific license to Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY) — the right fit for course content (vs. MIT/AGPL, which are software licenses). Worth aligning this summary's "MIT or cloud-adapted MIT" language with that finding during review.

Platform Strategy — Open edX vs. LMS

Why Open edX, and the long-term marketplace vision
Flexibility of Open edX (OeX)
Highlighted for its accessibility and flexibility — custom course pages or external links can be embedded directly into the platform shell rather than forcing a full migration.
Marketplace / e-commerce vision
Long-term vision: turn cityux.edu into an e-commerce marketplace for non-degree offerings, digital badges, industry certifications, and continuing-education (CE) modules.

Revenue Streams & Offerings

Tiered income model — short, mid, and long term
Short-term / internal
  • Service-based support
  • Data sharing with marketing
  • Training for instructors
Mid-term
  • Non-degree workshops
  • Digital badges
  • Compliance training
  • Industry certifications/licenses (e.g. thermal energy control, healthcare, engineering)
Long-term / external (B2C & B2B)
  • Premium courses
  • Marketplace content
  • External organizational partnerships

First Responders & Police Department Initiative

Academic-partner framing, not a vendor pitch
Academic & research partner role
For first responders (e.g. Seattle PD), CityU's role is to act as an academic and research partner — framing AI integration through critical-thinking frameworks like Bloom's Taxonomy and flipped classrooms, rather than a software sale.
Workshop series
A 5-part workshop series paired with seminars, to train and upskill personnel on AI compliance and tools.
