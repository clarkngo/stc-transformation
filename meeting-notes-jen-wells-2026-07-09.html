Making the Ecosystem Model Pluggable

Open question
How do we build the way we brainstormed and constructed the briefing — and the practitioner-publisher ecosystem itself — so other departments can plug into it, rather than it staying a one-off STC exercise? Is the right shape a framework (a repeatable method other schools follow) or a platform (shared infrastructure/tooling other schools use directly)?
Specific case
Applies directly to the staff/industry practitioner-publisher model — the mechanism only works institution-wide if other schools' faculty and staff can adopt it without needing STC to build it for them each time.
Underlying question
How do we make the change itself accessible — lowering the barrier for another department to try this, not just documenting that it worked once for STC.
Status: raised as an open design question, not yet resolved — flagged here for scoping in a follow-up (framework vs. platform decision, plus what a minimum "starter kit" for another department would need to contain).
🍳

Presentation Feedback: "Cooking Show" Structure

Core note
When explaining or briefing, structure it like a cooking show: show the finished dish first, then go back and divide it into its components/ingredients — rather than building up to the result step by step.
Why it lands
Leads with the payoff so the audience immediately sees what they're getting, then the breakdown of parts becomes context for something they've already bought into — instead of a list of ingredients whose purpose isn't clear until the end.
Apply going forward
Use this structure for future briefings and roundtable-style presentations: open with the finished outcome (the "plated dish"), then unpack the components that built it.
✅
