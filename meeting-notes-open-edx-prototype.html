Institutional Dynamics

Proposal
A new department is being proposed, potentially named the Office of AI Experience and Innovation, to house an agile micro-credentials and workforce-training ecosystem.
Leadership
Key stakeholders hold differing views on the proposal:
President

Very positive toward the concept — already considering position structures and budgeting.

Provost

Doubtful and reserved — requires clear evidence or an established ROI before fully supporting the shift toward micro-credentials, and is pessimistic about direct revenue generation, raising operational questions on platform hosting and tech costs.

🎯

Core Objective & Revenue Strategy

Demo Case
To secure institutional buy-in and funding, the goal is to build a small, functional "demo case" (prototype) of the ecosystem using an open-source platform — specifically Open edX — rather than relying purely on abstract concepts or slide presentations.
Revenue
The ultimate goal is to prove the infrastructure can host micro-credentials, industrial certifications, and advanced workforce training to generate sustainable revenue streams and boost university enrollment.
📋

Two-Week Assignment & Deliverables

1. Local Prototype Deployment

Open edX is already installed via Docker locally. Next step: continue studying the platform and ensure its stability as a baseline environment.

2. Hosting Research — AWS

Research minimum server configurations needed to run a small-scale Open edX demo environment on AWS, the most familiar cloud environment.

3. Domain Selection

Factor in purchase of a standard domain name (~$15) to point to the temporary cloud environment.

4. Cloud Access Handover

Prepare to give leadership access to the AWS account environment next week, once their own account details have been audited.

Budget
Keep the budget minimal — a remaining $600 research grant will cover temporary hosting fees, with an absolute maximum budget of $1,000.
