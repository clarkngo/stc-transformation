Proposed Curriculum Model: AI-Empowered Portfolio

Short description: This document contains the current curriculum model (for reference) followed by the proposed AI‑Empowered portfolio model. Use this combined view to compare vulnerabilities and proposed mitigations.

Current Curriculum Model: Traditional & High-Activity

Philosophy: Maximize engagement and provide multi-modal scaffolding through frequent, diverse, graded assignments (pre-AI design).

Status: High administrative overhead (QA Strain) and high vulnerability to Generative AI.

Graded Component Breakdown (~100%)

Exams / Knowledge Checks (~40-50%)

Focus: Conceptual Recall. Vulnerability: High risk for remote AI cheating.

Team Project (~20-30%)

Focus: Final product delivery. Vulnerability: Moderate (AI assists in code/report writing).

Programming Exercises (PE) (~10-20%)

Focus: Code practice. Vulnerability: High (AI Agents can provide direct solutions).

HOS (Hands-on Skills) (~10-20%)

Focus: Guided practice. Vulnerability: High (AI automates completion). Note: Grading this requires significant Student Worker time.

Muddiest Point (MP) & Concept Debate (CD) (~5-10%)

Focus: Weekly check-in/discussion. Vulnerability: Extreme (AI generates high-quality, deceptive responses).

Key Challenges (The "Root Cause")

  • QA Gridlock: Too many unique graded items (MP, CD, HOS) create massive strain on the QA process for compliance and alignment.
  • Misleading Metrics: High completion rates do not reflect genuine student effort or mastery due to AI assistance.
  • Student Worker Drain: Resources are spent grading low-value tasks instead of improving content.

Graded Component Breakdown (100%)

Applied Case Studies / Conceptual Assessment (40%)

Focus: Conceptual Mastery. Mitigation: Utilizes personalized, niche, and multi-modal prompts. Integrates mandatory Oral Defense (Viva) sessions for integrity.

Team Project: Architecture & Portfolio (40%)

Focus: System Design. Requires a formal portfolio of design documents, architecture choices, and a final product defense. Assesses complexity and design, not just functioning code.

Programming Exercises (PE) (20%)

Focus: AI-Aided Development. Requires students to submit a reflection log detailing Prompt Engineering and debugging of AI-generated code. Assesses management, not code production.

Non-Graded Components (Scaffolding & Support)

HOS (Hands-on Skills) (0%)

Role: Mandatory prerequisite practice. Efficiency: Student Workers are reassigned from grading this to curriculum review and improvement.

Concept/Project Discussion Forums (0%)

Role: Repurposed for mandatory Project Architecture discussions, peer feedback, and instructor-led Q&A.
