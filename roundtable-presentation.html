🐸
The Frog Incident — Heber City, UT

An AI-generated police report stated the officer "shapeshifted into a frog." The document was submitted, signed, and became unusable in court. This is what unreviewed AI output looks like in the legal record.

The evidence that changed our pitch

Adams et al. (2024) — a pre-registered, independent RCT testing Axon's Draft One on 85 officers across 755 real reports — found no statistically significant reduction in report-writing time. The 60-second vendor claim doesn't hold up under scrutiny. SPD's own senior research scientist raised this study at the June 16 meeting, unprompted. We pivoted from efficiency to accountability.

First Responders Research

Operational Guardrails: The Traffic Light Framework

A three-tier framework governing how first responders may use AI tools — from approved daily use to strictly banned.

Full Presentation ↗
Green — Approved Use
  • Fleet routing & scheduling
  • Bulk evidence processing (with human verification)
  • Translated public safety announcements
  • Administrative scheduling & resource allocation
Yellow — Pilot / Verified Only
  • BWC summarization for training only
  • Draft incident reports with mandatory manual redlining
  • Requires supervisor sign-off before any official use
Red — Strictly Banned
  • AI-written charging documents
  • Signing anything under penalty of perjury without full human verification
  • Running unvetted consumer AI on CJIS or health data
State-endorsed framework

The WA State AI Task Force formally adopted the NIST AI Risk Management Framework. The Traffic Light maps directly to its risk tiers. Same structural logic as the EU AI Act (Unacceptable / High / Minimal Risk). CityU's first responders micro-credential curriculum teaches this framework — Module 4 of the AI for First Responders course.

The compliance pilot — immediate ask

Every terminal operator in Seattle/Tacoma must prove role-specific cyber training under USCG 33 CFR 101.650 — effective this month. No regional provider is positioned to deliver this. CityU's solution: a 3-course professional certificate in Maritime OT Security, training Port "Key Personnel" to become designated Cybersecurity Officers (CySO). Funding via Port's 2026 Professional Development Scholarship. Contact: Port CIO Matt Breed.

Port of Seattle context

Rhysida ransomware attack — August 2024. 90,000 individuals affected; 71,000 WA residents. Compromised: names, DOBs, SSNs, driver's license numbers. Port refused to pay ransom. A maritime expert subsequently requested a briefing on CityU's Blue Tech research. The door is open.

🔲 The Uncertainty Matrix

The matrix we built isn't just a diagnostic — it's an argument. The questions we can't answer yet aren't gaps. They're the frontier. Teams that wait for certainty will miss the moment.

📊
Probabilistic Strategy

Forecast the most likely future, then optimize for it. Works when the range of outcomes is known and stable.

⚠️ Breaks down when AI changes the rules faster than forecasts can track.
🧪
Exploratory Strategy

Test multiple directions simultaneously, stay adaptive, let real-world signals guide the next move.

✅ The right tool for an AI era where the landscape shifts faster than any plan.
🧭
Direction Over Destination

In 1850, settlers heading west didn't have a precise route — they had a direction and a reason. They figured out the path as conditions revealed themselves.

AI strategy today is the same: a compass beats a GPS. CityU's direction is clear — workforce-ready, skills-first, open, and regionally embedded. The precise route? We discover it by moving.
